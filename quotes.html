Here are some thoughts from the past two years of SAS Reach students, along with the questions they're answering.

Levi Pettis (2023)

It is a rewarding learning opportunity, network with amazing people in STEM, ask questions and have a great time. Thank you again SAS!

Sahil Sudame (2023)
How are you taking advantage of the opportunities presented in the program? Have any new relationships benefited you?

Taken more time to delve into Viya 4 and the SAS language. Still meet with 3 other people outside of the program

Current SAS Intern
Armando Hernandez-Cruz (2023)
What would you like people to know about SAS Reach or your experiences with the program?

I think a program like SAS Reach is only as great as you make it for yourself. The coordinators put in a ton of effort to open doors for the students, but it is up to the student to take advantage of the people that they are able to bring into the room. Be open to learning, I developed some very important soft skills while I was with the SAS Reach group. As somebody who had a really bad stutter, I made it a goal to talk as much as I could to practice and get rid of my nervousness and insecurity around the way I spoke. Now I feel like the most confident and best version of myself possible. Again, a program like SAS Reach is amazing and puts so many opportunities in front of you but it is up to the students to be proactive with those opportunities.

Current SAS Intern
Cintya J Espinoza Segueda (2023)
What would you like people to know about SAS Reach or your experiences with the program?

Apply. It's going to be scary applying to internships or jobs, especially dealing with interviews, but you do yourself a disservice if you don't try. I would recommend anyone to apply regardless of how capable they felt. You'll more than likely realize you are way more able than you think. Sometimes it takes others seeing something in you for you to see it too. Also, don't ever let rejection make you feel uncapable. It is chance to see where you can grow.

Sallamatta Jallow (2024)
Do you believe a more traditional internship would have been more beneficial?

A non-traditional internship, like the SAS Reach program allows more room for growth in my opinion. In this internship I was exposed to many different occupations and positions within the company. Coming into this program there was nothing I NEEDED to know, everything was about being open minded and willing to learn. This experience was more beneficial as someone who didn't know a lot about the professional world.

William Palacios (2024)

These lessons have significantly benefited me, both during the program and in the long run.

What would you like people to know about SAS Reach or your experiences with the program?
